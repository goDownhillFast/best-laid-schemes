What makes it so hard to organize time?

(1) Time management is forward thinking. Most time management programs place a heavy emphasis on the planning stage, with
very little time left for the analysis stage.
(2) We are generally worried about the future
(3) We generally regret or extol the past
(4) We live in the present and future

Planning turns an envisioned future into an actual one.

However, time management plans do not react very well to changing conditions. If your wife had a terrible day, you may
not go to a piano lesson. If your friend has to work late, you may cancel dinner. You expect to work on one issue at
work, but another pops up and becomes much more pressing. While we can control much of what we do, a lot of our lives
are unplannable.
